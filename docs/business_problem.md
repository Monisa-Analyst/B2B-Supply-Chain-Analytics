# Business Problem Definition

## Context
This project focuses on the B2B sales register of a manufacturing supplier (Anand Engineering) over a 2.5-year operational period. Traditional ERP exports (Tally ERP) typically present transactions in a raw, ledger-based layout. This format makes it difficult to run complex queries, monitor recurring customer behavior, and evaluate product portfolio health at scale.

## Identified Challenges

### 1. High Customer Concentration
Manufacturing suppliers often depend on a very small set of key accounts to drive the majority of their revenue. Without formal concentration mapping, it is difficult to quantify the potential financial risk if a major customer reduces order volumes or switches to a competitor.

### 2. SKU Portfolio Volatility
With 248 active products across various categories, the company lacks visibility into SKU velocity. Slow-moving, low-margin products consume warehouse space and working capital, while high-velocity items need consistent replenishment monitoring to avoid stockouts.

### 3. Customer Churn and Retention Intervals
Unlike subscription-based business models, B2B purchasing is irregular and transactional. Identifying at-risk customers who have stopped purchasing requires recency tracking that traditional ledger exports do not natively provide.
